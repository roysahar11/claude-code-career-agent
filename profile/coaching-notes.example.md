### The Offer That Got Away

**What Happened**:
- Passed all interviews at Company A, received contract
- Friend invited you to interview at Company B for a better-titled role
- Delayed signing Company A's contract to explore Company B
- Company B went with a more experienced candidate
- Company A withdrew offer — hesitation was read as lack of motivation

**What Came Across Well**:
- Creative and analytical thinking
- Multidisciplinarity
- Genuine, polite, good person

**What Didn't Come Across**:
- Clear motivation for the job (was internally processing emotions)

**Key Lessons**:
1. Communicate openly during offer stage — silence reads as hesitation
2. "Technically possible" does not equal "advisable" — urgency requests have real reasons
3. Project motivation even while processing — internal uncertainty shouldn't show externally
4. Watch the hesitation pattern — wanting to keep all options open can backfire

**Resolution**:
How you handled the aftermath — maturity, self-awareness, professional resolution.

## Behavioral Patterns to Watch

<!-- Identify recurring patterns that affect your job search. The agent uses these to coach you proactively. -->

### Pattern Name
<!-- Example:

### The Hesitation Pattern

**What it looks like**:
- Wanting to keep all options open
- Fear of closing doors or missing opportunities
- Difficulty making bold choices in one direction
- Can appear as lack of motivation or commitment to others

**Why it happens**:
- Many interests — doesn't want to lose any
- First full-time job feels like a big commitment
- Processing emotions internally without communicating externally

**How to address it**:
- Working on this through personal development (therapy, mindfulness, etc.)
- Agent should watch for signs of this pattern
- Gently call it out when it appears
- Remind: first job is stepping stone, not permanent prison
- Help clarify motivation BEFORE interviews so it comes through naturally
-->
